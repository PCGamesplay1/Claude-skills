## Quick Reference: The Visual Architecture (9 Rules)

1. **Planimetric Composition** — Camera perpendicular to scene, flat 2D layers, horizontal/vertical movement only
2. **Obsessive Symmetry** — Primary subject dead center, elements mirrored on both sides
3. **Face-on Framing** — Characters face camera directly or perfectly in profile, never three-quarter
4. **Miniature Diorama Aesthetic** — Tactile materials: wood, wool, corduroy, translucent resin
5. **Specific Analogous Color Palettes** — Unified scheme: soft pastels (pinks/baby blues) or saturated autumn (mustard/orange/brick red)
6. **Overhead Knolling** — Top-down, 90-degree grid of objects, uniform spacing
7. **Deep-Focus Tableau** — Everything foreground to background in sharp focus
8. **Cinematic Aspect Ratios** — 1.37:1 Academy or 2.40:1 anamorphic
9. **Typography as Branding** — Centered title cards, bold serif fonts (Futura, Tilda), storybook feel

## Part II: Color Psychology Formulas

```
The Signature Palette: (1 Dominant Color) + (2–3 Saturated Analogous Shades) = A Vivid Cinematic Microcosm
The Emotional Subversion: (Bright Candy Pastels) + (Dark/Tragic Event) = Quirky Off-Kilter Personality
The Character Brand: (Core Personality Trait) + (1 Saturated Primary Color) = Visual Identity
```

**Character color mapping:**
- Red → trauma, suffering (Chas Tenenbaum tracksuit)
- Blue → intellectual calm
- Purple → luxury seeking
- Yellow → central identity (yellow submarine, yellow tent)

**Color shift as narrative**: Character losing their way → shift to black/white or grey.

## Part III: Stop-Motion & Miniatures

**Formulas:**
```
The Breathing Puppet: (Shooting on Twos) + (Ruffled "Boiling" Fur) = Handcrafted Vitality
The Scaled Epic: (Hero-Scale 12–13") + (Fingernail-Scale for wide shots) = Cinematic Depth
The Tactile Illusion: (Real Mohair/Alpaca) + (Lead-Lined Fabrics) + (Carved Soap Smoke) = Analog Magic
```

**Shooting on Twos**: 12fps instead of 24fps — gives the "crunchy" jitter that signals handcrafted.  
**Boiling**: Shimmering ripple from animator's fingers touching fur between shots — kept deliberately.

## Part IV: Editing Rhythm

```
The Anderson Groove: (Static Shot A) + (Music Beat) + (90° Snap or Whip Pan) + (Static Shot B) = Snappy Narrative Flow
The Emotional Anchor: (Lingering Long Take) + (Slow-Motion) + (Subtle Gesture) = Poignant Drama
The Chapter Header: (Solid Color Background) + (Bold Centered Typography) + (Humorous Info) = Storybook Introduction
```

**Compass Point Editing**: Camera moves only at 90° or 180° increments — clicks into positions like a clock face.
